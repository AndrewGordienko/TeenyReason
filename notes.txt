The crawler runs short episodes with simple probing behavior. At first that can just be random actions, but slightly better than pure random is a small library of probes:
hold one action, alternate actions, short bursts, long bursts, low-noise wandering. That gives better coverage than white-noise randomness.

For every step, record tuples like:

(s_t, a_t, s_{t+1}, done)

and also short windows:

(s_{t-k:t}, a_{t-k:t-1})

because some environments only reveal physics over multiple steps, not one-step transitions.
